## 8. SICKNESS AND ABSENCE

If you are unable to attend work due to illness or injury, you must inform the nursery manager (or a nominated deputy) by telephone before your normal start time on the first day of absence.

For absences of up to seven calendar days, you must complete a self-certification form on your return. For absences of eight or more days, you must provide a fit note from a GP.

You are entitled to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) as set out in current legislation, subject to meeting the qualifying conditions. The Employer does not currently operate an enhanced sick pay scheme beyond SSP.

## 13. SAFEGUARDING

You acknowledge that your role involves working with children. You are required at all times to:

- Act in accordance with *Keeping Children Safe in Education* and EYFS safeguarding guidance
- Complete all required safeguarding training within the timescales set by the Employer
- Report any concern about a child's welfare to the Designated Safeguarding Lead immediately
- Cooperate with any child protection investigation

Failure to meet your safeguarding obligations will be treated as a serious disciplinary matter and may constitute gross misconduct.

Your employment is conditional on an enhanced DBS check with barred list check remaining clear. The Employer may suspend or terminate your employment pending investigation if a concern arises.
