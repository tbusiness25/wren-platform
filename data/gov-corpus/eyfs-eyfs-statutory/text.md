# EYFS Statutory Framework 2024

**Source:** ladn.framework_statements (51 statements)
**Category:** eyfs  
**Audience:** both  

---


## Communication and Language


### Listening, Attention and Understanding

**End of Reception**: **[ELG01a]** Listen attentively and respond to what they hear with relevant questions, comments and actions when being read to and during whole class discussions and small group interactions.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG01b]** Make comments about what they have heard and ask questions to clarify their understanding.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG01c]** Hold conversation when engaged in back-and-forth exchanges with their teacher and peers.


### Speaking

**End of Reception**: **[ELG02a]** Participate in small group, class and one-to-one discussions, offering their own ideas, using recently introduced vocabulary.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG02b]** Offer explanations for why things might happen, making use of recently introduced vocabulary from stories, non-fiction, rhymes and poems when appropriate.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG02c]** Express their ideas and feelings about their experiences using full sentences, including use of past, present and future tenses and making use of conjunctions, with modelling and support from their teacher.


## Personal, Social and Emotional Development


### Self-Regulation

**End of Reception**: **[ELG03a]** Show an understanding of their own feelings and those of others, and begin to regulate their behaviour accordingly.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG03b]** Set and work towards simple goals, being able to wait for what they want and control their immediate impulses when appropriate.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG03c]** Give focused attention to what the teacher says, responding appropriately even when engaged in activity, and show an ability to follow instructions involving several ideas or actions.


### Managing Self

**End of Reception**: **[ELG04a]** Be confident to try new activities and show independence, resilience and perseverance in the face of challenge.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG04b]** Explain the reasons for rules, know right from wrong and try to behave accordingly.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG04c]** Manage their own basic hygiene and personal needs, including dressing, going to the toilet and understanding the importance of healthy food choices.


### Building Relationships

**End of Reception**: **[ELG05a]** Work and play cooperatively and take turns with others.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG05b]** Form positive attachments to adults and friendships with peers.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG05c]** Show sensitivity to their own and to others' needs.


## Physical Development


### Gross Motor Skills

**End of Reception**: **[ELG06a]** Negotiate space and obstacles safely, with consideration for themselves and others.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG06b]** Demonstrate strength, balance and coordination when playing.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG06c]** Move energetically, such as running, jumping, dancing, hopping, skipping and climbing.


### Fine Motor Skills

**End of Reception**: **[ELG07a]** Hold a pencil effectively in preparation for fluent writing – using the tripod grip in almost all cases.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG07b]** Use a range of small tools, including scissors, paintbrushes and cutlery.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG07c]** Begin to show accuracy and care when drawing.


## Literacy


### Comprehension

**End of Reception**: **[ELG08a]** Demonstrate understanding of what has been read to them by retelling stories and narratives using their own words and recently introduced vocabulary.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG08b]** Anticipate – where appropriate – key events in stories.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG08c]** Use and understand recently introduced vocabulary during discussions about stories, non-fiction, rhymes and poems and during role-play.


### Word Reading

**End of Reception**: **[ELG09a]** Say a sound for each letter in the alphabet and at least 10 digraphs.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG09b]** Read words consistent with their phonic knowledge by sound-blending.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG09c]** Read aloud simple sentences and books that are consistent with their phonic knowledge, including some common exception words.


### Writing

**End of Reception**: **[ELG10a]** Write recognisable letters, most of which are correctly formed.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG10b]** Spell words by identifying sounds in them and representing the sounds with a letter or letters.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG10c]** Write simple phrases and sentences that can be read by others.


## Mathematics


### Number

**End of Reception**: **[ELG11a]** Have a deep understanding of number to 10, including the composition of each number.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG11b]** Subitise (recognise quantities without counting) up to 5.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG11c]** Automatically recall (without reference to rhymes, counting or other aids) number bonds up to 5 (including subtraction facts) and some number bonds to 10, including double facts.


### Numerical Patterns

**End of Reception**: **[ELG12a]** Verbally count beyond 20, recognising the pattern of the counting system.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG12b]** Compare quantities up to 10 in different contexts, recognising when one quantity is greater than, less than or the same as the other quantity.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG12c]** Explore and represent patterns within numbers up to 10, including evens and odds, double facts and how quantities can be distributed equally.


## Understanding the World


### Past and Present

**End of Reception**: **[ELG13a]** Talk about the lives of the people around them and their roles in society.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG13b]** Know some similarities and differences between things in the past and now, drawing on their experiences and what has been read in class.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG13c]** Understand the past through settings, characters and events encountered in books read in class and storytelling.


### People, Culture and Communities

**End of Reception**: **[ELG14a]** Describe their immediate environment using knowledge from observation, discussion, stories, non-fiction texts and maps.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG14b]** Know some similarities and differences between different religious and cultural communities in this country, drawing on their experiences and what has been read in class.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG14c]** Explain some similarities and differences between life in this country and life in other countries, drawing on knowledge from stories, non-fiction texts and – when appropriate – maps.


### The Natural World

**End of Reception**: **[ELG15a]** Explore the natural world around them, making observations and drawing pictures of animals and plants.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG15b]** Know some similarities and differences between the natural world around them and contrasting environments, drawing on their experiences and what has been read in class.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG15c]** Understand some important processes and changes in the natural world around them, including the seasons and changing states of matter.


## Expressive Arts and Design


### Creating with Materials

**End of Reception**: **[ELG16a]** Safely use and explore a variety of materials, tools and techniques, experimenting with colour, design, texture, form and function.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG16b]** Share their creations, explaining the process they have used.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG16c]** Make use of props and materials when role playing characters in narratives and stories.


### Being Imaginative and Expressive

**End of Reception**: **[ELG17a]** Invent, adapt and recount narratives and stories with peers and their teacher.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG17b]** Sing a range of well-known nursery rhymes and songs.

**End of Reception**: **[ELG17c]** Perform songs, rhymes, poems and stories with others, and – when appropriate – try to move in time with music.
