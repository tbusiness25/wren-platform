Method & assumptions — read before quoting a number

  • Ratios: Baby room 1:3 (under-2s). Pre-school modelled at 1:6 — a blend of your actual DOB mix (~46% two-year-olds at 1:5, ~54% three-plus at 1:8 → ~1:6.3; 1:6 is slightly stricter, so it under-states surplus).
  • Staff count = distinct adults rostered to a room that day (a full-day person counted once; anyone struck-through excluded). Because shifts stagger (7:45–2 / 1:30–6), fewer are present at any instant than the daily headcount — so as a simultaneous-ratio check this over-states staffing, but as a labour-cost unit (each rostered shift is paid) it is the right measure.
  • Not every rostered adult counts toward ratio (apprentice, SENCO doing 1:1, room leader on admin/PPA, someone covering the kitchen). That legitimately lifts required cover — the "+1/room buffer" figure is designed to absorb most of it. Treat the min+1 column as the realistic opportunity, not the raw minimum column.
  • Cost: £13.84/hr avg floor-staff gross (practitioner £12.98, deputy £18.33, apprentice £9, from the staff table) × 6.6 h avg paid day × 1.12 loaded on-costs.
  • Data: hand-transcribed from 31 photographed weekly plans by AI OCR; child counts were clear, some strikethroughs/covers were judgement calls (flagged in the source notes). Christmas week, Good Friday, Easter Monday, the Spring bank holiday and the Feb INSET day are excluded as closed; w/c 29 Dec had no daily counts and was dropped entirely.
  • Annualised = observed 31-week figure × 1.61 to ~50 operating weeks. An estimate, not a forecast.

Net: don't quote "£60,360 wasted" — that's against a floor you can't actually run at. The defensible headline is ~£48,678/yr of over-provision beyond a comfortable buffer, mostly in pre-school and mostly on quieter days (Fridays). That's the pot worth trimming — ideally by flexing quiet-day/Friday cover, not cutting posts.
