Some of you may have seen from our signs that we were also celebrating Charlie's 10-year anniversary with us. Congratulations to her on a wonderful achievement. She's come such a long way since she joined, and we feel incredibly lucky to have a team that actually wants to stay for the long haul.

Low staff turnover is the biggest hurdle the Early Years sector faces right now. Too often this work is unfairly dismissed as a babysitting job or something people do for a bit until they find a "real career". Because of low wages and physical demand, it's genuinely hard to find people who are truly passionate about it and want to stay.

That's why Charlie's 10 years — and our current team's combined 145 years — is so massive. Nationally, the average nursery staff member stays for only 2 or 3 years; ours is nearly 10 per staff member. 

I also want to say a huge thank you to our senior staff. This level of stability doesn't happen by accident. They do an incredible job of running great teams, mentoring our newer practitioners, and making the nursery somewhere people want to build a career. We're very proud of the "work family" they've built here.

Our New Parents Portal

I've always kept an eye on tech and I'm fairly handy with computers. Over the past year or two I've watched the early years sector follow the same pattern as everyone else; more cloud, more AI, more of your children's data sitting on someone else's server.

There's a small but real gap in the market for doing it the other way: keeping everything offline, on-site, on our own hardware. That's what I've been working on currently. Our new portal runs on a dedicated PC here at the nursery, fully locked down. Parents connect through encrypted tunnels — no open ports, no third-party cloud, no data ever leaving the building. Every tablet on the nursery floor has its own cryptographic key that can be revoked instantly if it's leaves the setting. Every login and every change is logged and audited.

I think its been relatively easy to do as I know exactly what our needs are. Part of it is about saving money; we currently pay subscriptions to nine separate vendors, none of which talk to each other. But the bigger reason is having everything in one place, under our control, and not having to trust another company with your children's information after the nursery hacks we've seen in the news.

Everyday Reminders

Spare clothes — please make sure your child has an adequate amount of spare clothes in their yellow bag, including socks and underwear. If you do need to borrow nursery clothes, please remember to return them — we've had a high volume of clothes not coming back, and we'd rather not add additional paperwork to sign when clothes go home.

Blue nappy bag — if your child has a blue bag on their peg, this means we're running low on nappies. Please take the bag home, refill with nappies, and bring it in the next day.

Summer clothing — we'd recommend plenty of spare summer clothes, as there isn't always time on the washing line for them to re-wear. Appreciated!

Parents folder (resources & documents) — we encourage all families to make use of our parents folder. It has information for new parents (welcome letter, settling-in procedures, room timetables), our menus, our policies for illnesses, information on phonics, toileting, behaviour management and oral health, plus music and audiobooks (great for bedtime stories).

For now this is still accessible via the eyparent app: Home Learning → Resources & Documents → Parents Folder. The new look has made the folders a little hard to see — tap the grey info button to read the full folder name. We'll be moving all of these resources into our new parents portal over the coming weeks, so you'll have everything in one place.

Story sacks — please make use of our story sacks, which can be borrowed and taken home for the whole family to enjoy. We've recently updated many of them to reflect our children's favourite books. Baby Room families: please let a member of the team know you'd like to borrow a sack and we'll sign it out for you. Pre-school families: help yourselves, and sign the sacks in and out using the clipboard above — the sacks are on the pegs opposite the messy room, above the artwork pigeon holes.

Buggy Area

A gentle reminder to please collapse your buggies if you're using the buggy parking area. If you live close by, we'd also encourage Pre-school families to have your big angels walk, scoot or cycle in — it frees up more space for the babies' buggies, promotes being healthy, and prepares them for school readiness (and for our nursery trips to the park).

If you do use the buggy area, please push your collapsed buggy all the way in so it doesn't get wet in the rain. If you need to move other buggies to fit yours in, please keep them inside the enclosed area.
