Summer Term 2026 — what your child is learning this term

Summer 2 — Transport and Journeys

Baby Room · Capacity 10 · 1 June – 17 July 2026
We are led by the children in terms of activity planning and take our cue from what they show interest in. Each child's needs are met on an individual basis.

Learning Intentions

  • To share enjoyment with an adult during songs, stories or play about vehicles.
  • To listen to and begin to recognise transport sounds e.g. sirens, horns, etc.
  • To strengthen fine motor skills by grasping, rolling, and moving vehicles.
  • To notice real vehicles in the environment e.g. cars, bikes, buses, etc.
  • To explore mark making with toy vehicles in paint, sand, or gloop (tracks, patterns).

Summer 2 — Space

Pre-school · Capacity 22 · 1 June – 17 July 2026
Pre-school planning runs as a half-term topic with related activities explored across the weeks. Each activity is shown with our intent, how we'll implement it, the expected impact on children's learning, and the adult's role in supporting it.

Half-Term Activities

Transport and Journeys

13 April – 22 May 2026 (last half-term)
Wheel Printing
Intent

Explore transport through creative art.

How we'll do it

Provide toy cars with different wheels to dip in paint and roll across paper.

Expected impact

Children notice patterns, develop fine motor skills, and make creative choices.

Adult's role

Model printing, introduce words (tracks, wheels, roll, spin), and encourage descriptive talk.

Journey Maps
Intent

Support children's understanding of journeys and places.

How we'll do it

Provide large paper and pens to draw simple maps (home to nursery, park, holiday). Add stickers for roads, trees, houses.

Expected impact

Builds early mark making, awareness of place, and sequencing.

Adult's role

Model drawing roads/paths, ask questions ('What do we see on the way?'), and encourage storytelling.

Traffic Light Game
Intent

Develop listening skills, turn-taking, and awareness of road safety.

How we'll do it

Play a stop-and-go game: red = stop, green = go, amber = get ready. Children move around the space as different vehicles.

Expected impact

Builds coordination, listening, and awareness of road safety signs.

Adult's role

Model the actions, reinforce safety language (stop, wait, go), and encourage self-regulation.

Floating and Sinking Boats
Intent

Explore scientific concepts through water play.

How we'll do it

Provide different materials (plastic, foil, wood, sponge) for children to make boats. Test which float or sink.

Expected impact

Encourages prediction, observation, and problem-solving.

Adult's role

Model testing, introduce words (float, sink, heavy, light), and ask children to predict outcomes.

Transport Songs and Rhymes
Intent

Develop rhythm, memory, and language through transport songs.

How we'll do it

Sing rhymes like 'The Wheels on the Bus', 'Row, Row, Row Your Boat', and 'Down at the Station'. Add actions or props.

Expected impact

Children develop rhythm, recall, and confidence in joining group songs.

Adult's role

Lead singing, model actions, and praise children for joining in.

Space

1 June – 17 July 2026 (this half-term)
Rocket Building
Intent

Encourage creativity and problem-solving through design.

How we'll do it

Provide junk modelling materials (boxes, tubes, foil) for children to build rockets.

Expected impact

Develops imagination, construction skills, and teamwork.

Adult's role

Model joining materials, introduce words (launch, blast, orbit), and encourage children to describe their rockets.

Constellation Art
Intent

Introduce children to stars and patterns in the night sky.

How we'll do it

Use black paper and chalk/white paint dots to make constellations, linking them with lines.

Expected impact

Builds fine motor control, pattern recognition, and wonder about space.

Adult's role

Share simple constellation names, model how to join dots, and encourage storytelling about star shapes.

Moon Surface Exploration
Intent

Explore textures and sensory play linked to the moon.

How we'll do it

Use flour, sand, or clay to create a 'moon surface' tray. Hide small rocks or 'moon treasures' inside for digging.

Expected impact

Supports sensory exploration, descriptive language, and curiosity.

Adult's role

Model using tools, introduce words (crater, dusty, rocky), and encourage descriptive talk.

Alien Craft
Intent

Support imaginative thinking and creativity.

How we'll do it

Provide craft materials (googly eyes, pipe cleaners, collage shapes) for children to design their own aliens.

Expected impact

Develops fine motor skills, individuality, and storytelling.

Adult's role

Model designing, talk about features (antenna, eyes, colours), and encourage children to describe their aliens.

Planet Sorting — Size and Colour
Intent

Help children notice differences between planets.

How we'll do it

Provide planet cards or balls in different sizes/colours. Children sort them into big/small or group by colour.

Expected impact

Builds early maths (sorting, comparing) and science awareness.

Adult's role

Model sorting, use comparative language (bigger/smaller, red/blue), and extend with open questions ('Which planet is the biggest?').
